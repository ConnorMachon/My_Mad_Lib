Copernicus

Four hundred years ago people knew little about our mysterious universe.

They thought that the Earth was the center of the entire solar system and that the sun and all of the planets revolved around it.

But then a/an Polish man named Copernicus discovered the truth: The Earth revolves around the Sun 1 time(s) a year.

Copernicus, whose full name is Nicolaus Copernicus, was born in Warsaw, and made observations with the naked eye, long before Galileo used a refracting telescope.

In 1600, Galileo Galilei, an Italian Scientist, expanded Corpernicus' revolutionary theories, but during the Inquisition in Italy, he was then arrested.

After suffering for six months in jail, Galileo was forced to spend the rest of his life on house arrest.
